About (Re)Starting And Giving Up

_Date: March 6, 2024 | Author: Youkai Princess

Consistency

The act of keep moving on made me reach a place I've never seen, I just imagine how it would be like but I only realised when I looked back and said "wow, nice job girl!".

It was hard, it was consuming and I was thinking that I wasn't capable of reaching certain grade of quality on my work.

Until look back I was thinking that I've never be like those artists I follow and admire.

Burning Out

It was quick I would say. It took me 3 months to burn my mind out.

Of course, this period was the stress peak which made me had a burnout but the situation was going in this direction for a while before it.

I could understand how small and also big content creators have burnout frequently.

Well, I was in the same route they follow. I studied their business to establish the pillars for my own business.

Finding a Way

But how can I reach the audience/algorithm expectations for frequency and quality if it takes me around 30 hours to make a 3D model?

This idea of posting everyday for growth is even an idea?

Looking for a way to make things work for my side I found a interesting concept, at minimum. It was the kitbash, the art of packing 3D models in a way that you can disassemble parts to make a new model out of it or at least, speed up the process of making similar models.

I liked the idea so much that I call it *kittybashing*

Reaching Talent

When the situation demands, I can go well, even under pressure and not knowing the next steps I need to make.

I was compromised to make things faster so I changed my pipeline to accommodate this philosophy and it worked out even better than I expected!

At the first time I could have some control over the outcomes of my works. I planned to reach a deadline and I reached it without any problems.

It's All About Practice

This journey made me learn a lot about project management. This journey also made me see my whole career as a big life project.

Doing things faster than I can do resulted in delaying even more my goals, that's why I put my practice in first place now.

Practicing I could go way farther than I has been just producing.

Creating is important, but intention is what makes the creation something unique.
